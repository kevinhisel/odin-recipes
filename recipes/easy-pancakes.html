Easy Pancakes

Description

This is a recipe for homemade pancakes. I borrowed it from America's Test Kitchen, and I really enjoyed how easy this pancake batter was to put together.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 cups milk

Steps

Make the batter

  1. Whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt together in large bowl.
  2. Whisk eggs and ¼ cup oil in second medium bowl until well combined.
  3. Whisk milk and vanilla into egg mixture.
  4. Add egg mixture to flour mixture and stir gently until just combined (batter should remain lumpy with few streaks of flour).
  5. Let batter sit for 10 minutes before cooking.

Make a test pancake

  1. Heat ½ teaspoon oil in 12-inch nonstick skillet over medium-low heat until shimmering.
  2. Using paper towels, carefully wipe out oil, leaving thin film on bottom and sides of skillet.
  3. Drop 1 tablespoon batter in center of skillet.
  4. If pancake is pale golden brown after 1 minute, skillet is ready. If it is too light or too dark, adjust heat accordingly.

Cook the pancakes

  1. Using ¼-cup dry measuring cup, portion batter into skillet in 3 places, leaving 2 inches between portions. If necessary, gently spread batter into 4-inch round.
  2. Cook until edges are set, first sides are golden brown, and bubbles on surface are just beginning to break, 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Using thin, wide spatula, flip pancakes and continue to cook until second sides are golden brown, 1 to 2 minutes longer.
  4. Serve. Repeat with remaining batter, using remaining ½ teaspoon oil as necessary.
